Physics – Form 1: Introduction to Physics

1. What is Physics?

Physics is the scientific study of matter, energy, and the fundamental laws of nature. It helps us understand how the universe behaves — from tiny particles to massive galaxies.

  • Branches: Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electromagnetism, Optics, Modern Physics
  • Importance in KCSE: Foundation for all science subjects and real-world applications

2. Measurements & SI Units

Accurate measurement is essential in physics. We use the International System of Units (SI).

Quantity SI Unit Symbol

TemperaturekelvinK

3. Scalar vs Vector Quantities

Scalar: Has magnitude only (e.g., mass, time, speed, distance, temperature).

Vector: Has magnitude and direction (e.g., velocity, force, displacement, acceleration, weight).

  • Scalar addition: Simple arithmetic
  • Vector addition: Head-to-tail method or parallelogram law
